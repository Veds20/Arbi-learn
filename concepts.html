Web3 Fundamentals

Core Concepts, Decoded

Side-by-side comparisons that cut through the jargon. No textbook walls — just clear, visual understanding.

01 Web2 vs Web3
🌐 Web2
// The Internet You Know
🏢
Centralized control — Google, Meta, and Amazon own your data, your identity, and your content. They can delete your account, sell your data, or go offline.
🔑
Username + Password — Your identity is a database row owned by the platform. Forget your password? Pray to their support team.
💰
You are the product — Free apps generate revenue by harvesting and selling behavioral data. Every click is tracked.
🚫
Permissioned access — Platforms can ban you, censor content, or restrict API access at will. You have no recourse.
VS
⛓️ Web3
// The Ownership Internet
🌍
Decentralized networks — Thousands of nodes globally hold the data. No single company can shut it down or censor it.
🔐
Wallet = Identity — Your cryptographic keypair IS your identity. You own it. No company holds it. Recover with a 12-word seed phrase.
🪙
Tokenized ownership — Earn tokens for contributing. Own a piece of the protocols you use. Value flows to participants, not platforms.
✅
Permissionless — Smart contracts run on-chain. Anyone can interact, deploy, or fork. Code is law — no gatekeepers.
02 Ethereum vs Bitcoin
Property ₿ Bitcoin ⟠ Ethereum
Purpose Digital gold — a store of value and peer-to-peer payment system Programmable blockchain — a world computer for decentralized apps
Created 2009 by Satoshi Nakamoto (pseudonymous) 2015 by Vitalik Buterin and co-founders
Smart Contracts ❌ Not natively supported — script only, very limited ✅ Full Turing-complete smart contracts via Solidity/Vyper
Supply Hard capped at 21 million BTC — fixed forever by the protocol No hard cap — ~120M ETH supply, with burn mechanism (EIP-1559)
Consensus Proof of Work — miners compete to solve puzzles, energy intensive Proof of Stake — validators lock ETH as collateral, ~99.95% less energy
Block Time ~10 minutes per block ~12 seconds per block
Ecosystem Primarily payments, Lightning Network for speed, Ordinals for NFTs DeFi, NFTs, DAOs, L2s, gaming, identity — the broadest ecosystem in Web3
Analogy Digital gold — scarce, predictable, store of value Digital oil — powers computation, fuels the decentralized economy

Public Key

✅ Safe to share on Twitter, in emails, or on your website

Private Key
Your Password — Never Share
5HueCGU8rMjxECyDialwujkPMNSMQZiE★★★★★★★★★★★★★★★★
🗝️ Like your house key — whoever has it controls your wallet, period
🔏 256-bit random number — essentially impossible to brute-force guess
✍️ Used to sign transactions, proving you authorized them without revealing the key
⚠️ Lost private key = lost funds forever. No customer support. No recovery.
⚡ How it works together: You sign a transaction with your private key → the network verifies the signature using your public key → transaction is broadcast. The private key proves ownership without ever being revealed.
04 Blockchain vs Traditional Databases
🗄️ Traditional DB
// MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB
👤
Single admin — One entity controls read/write permissions. The DBA can modify or delete any record.
⚡
Extremely fast — Millions of reads/writes per second. Optimized for performance with indexes, caching, and sharding.
✏️
Mutable records — UPDATE and DELETE are core operations. History can be rewritten or wiped.
🏛️
Trust the operator — You trust the company running the database to be honest, secure, and available.
VS
⛓️ Blockchain
// Ethereum, Bitcoin, Arbitrum
🌍
Distributed consensus — Thousands of nodes must agree. No single entity can alter the data without controlling 51%+ of the network.
🐢
Slower but trustless — Speed is sacrificed for decentralization. Every node verifies every transaction independently.
🔒
Immutable history — Once confirmed, data cannot be changed. Every block links to the previous via cryptographic hash.
🤖
Trust the math — Cryptography and consensus rules guarantee integrity. No need to trust any human operator.
