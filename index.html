The Arbitrum Story

Why Ethereum needed Layer 2

Ethereum is the world's programmable blockchain — but popularity came at a cost.

01 / THE PROBLEM
Ethereum's Scalability Trilemma

Ethereum prioritizes security and decentralization — but that means sacrificing speed. At peak usage, a single swap could cost $50–$200 in gas fees and take minutes to confirm. This made DeFi, NFTs, and gaming practically unusable for everyday people.

02 / THE SOLUTION
What Arbitrum Does

Arbitrum is an Optimistic Rollup — it batches thousands of transactions off-chain, compresses them, and posts a single proof to Ethereum mainnet. You get Ethereum's security guarantees at a fraction of the cost. Transactions settle in under a second, fees drop to cents, and existing Ethereum smart contracts deploy with zero changes.

03 / REAL BENEFIT
Arbitrum vs Mainnet in Practice

Swapping $100 of ETH on mainnet: ~$15 fee, ~30 second wait. Same swap on Arbitrum One: ~$0.04 fee, ~0.3 second confirmation. The same dApps, same wallet, same security — just 400x cheaper and 100x faster. That's why Arbitrum hosts billions in DeFi TVL.
